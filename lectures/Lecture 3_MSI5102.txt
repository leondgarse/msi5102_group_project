▪ Consider two probability distributions p, q on finite Y.
▪ The entropy of the distribution p is defined as:
𝐻 𝑝 = − σ𝐾
𝑖=1 𝑝(𝑦𝑖 )log 𝑝 𝑦𝑖
▪ The cross entropy of p relative to q is defined as:

𝑖=1 𝑞(𝑦𝑖 )log 𝑝 𝑦𝑖
•

Assume
-- p is the true distribution
-- q is the predicted distribution
• Cross-entropy measures how hard it is to use one
probability distribution q to encode data that is actually
generated from another distribution p
• If q is close to p → cross-entropy is small
If q is far from p → cross-entropy is large

Summary
▪ A point predictor makes a single guess of y, given x
▪ A probabilistic predictor guesses a probability distribution on Y, given x
▪ We assess a probabilistic classifier by its average log-likelihood on test data
